# House With the Switches

- Room title: `House With the Switches`
- Stable ID: `room.main_floor.house_with_the_switches`

## Status

Draft - Pending Gemini Notebook review before any implementation wave.

## Emotional Thesis

The room explores the fear of trying to restore order by force and learning that stability only returns when the player stops treating every problem like a thing to overpower.

## Player-Facing Goal

Restore the house to a livable state by understanding why ordinary control fails here, seeing what the harsh light hides, and returning the room to a stable, breathable state.

## Ordinary Baseline

At first glance, this is a modest domestic room with one ordinary wall switch, familiar overhead lighting, and the expectation that a simple flip should restore order.

## Distortion Rule

When the player flips the switch in Ego Form while the internalized Father rule is dominant, the room answers with fracture instead of relief: light splinters, pressure spikes, and the space becomes less stable. This is not a timing, sequence, or combination problem. It is a wrong-self-state problem governed by the room's emotional law.

## Pressure Source

Escalating sensory instability caused by repeated wrong-form interaction: forced Ego control under surveillance, light fracture, visual unease, and a growing sense that the room is resisting command rather than being repaired.

## Safe Pocket

A small grounded area near the room entrance remains visually consistent and becomes the place where the player can pause, observe, and reorient before trying again. Optional later polish: a faint Snow Owl white-feather shimmer or subtle audio motif could mark this pocket as an orientation anchor without creating new system obligations.

## Shadow Reveal

The reveal is not about a broken circuit. When the player allows darkness and enters Shadow perception, the overhead institutional glare stops dictating the room and a hidden architectural lie becomes visible: a crack, seam, route, or connection that ordinary light conceals. Shadow here is not evil, demonic, or superhero detective vision. It is the vulnerable perception state that can see what control refuses to admit.

## Puzzle Beats

1. Approach
   The player enters expecting a straightforward “flip the switch and make the room behave” problem and notices familiar domestic cues that invite ordinary control.
2. False Solution
   An initial Ego-form interaction suggests the switch should solve the problem if the player just insists hard enough.
3. Wrong-Form Feedback
   When the player tries to force an Ego/control solution under surveillance, the room answers with instability: the light fractures, pressure spikes, and the space becomes less trustworthy instead of more obedient.
4. Reveal
   The player stops insisting on light as safety, enters Shadow perception, and sees what the overhead bulbs hide: the true seam, route, or connection that reframes the room.
5. Stabilize
   With the hidden truth now visible, the player can stop escalating the room and begin restoring a more coherent state.
6. Integrated Action
   The player completes one integrated action in which Shadow reveals the true path and Ego restores structure. Neither state solves the room alone.
7. Repair/Return
   The room settles back into an ordinary state, confirming that it can be lived in again rather than merely conquered.
